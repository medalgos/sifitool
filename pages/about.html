About MedAlgos

Our Mission

MedAlgos provides reliable, peer-reviewed, and easy-to-use medical tools for clinical practice. Our mission is to support healthcare providers in delivering evidence-based care through accessible digital tools.

The tools we develop are:

  • Evidence-based: Built on the latest clinical guidelines and peer-reviewed research
  • User-friendly: Designed with clinical workflow in mind for point-of-care use
  • Regularly updated: Maintained to reflect current medical practice
  • Free and accessible: Available to all healthcare professionals without barriers

Development Process

Each calculator undergoes a rigorous development process:

  1. Literature Review: Comprehensive review of current clinical guidelines and literature
  2. Algorithm Development: Creation of computational algorithms by medical professionals
  3. Peer Review: Review by specialists in the relevant field
  4. Beta Testing: User testing in clinical environments
  5. Deployment: Release to the medical community
  6. Maintenance: Regular updates based on new evidence and user feedback

Development Team

MedAlgos is the result of a collaborative effort between infectious disease doctors and data scientists. Together, we’re combining medical expertise with cutting-edge technology to create tools that make a real difference in clinical practice.

Our team brings diverse skills to the table, including:

  • Pediatrics and Neonatology
  • Internal Medicine
  • Infectious Disease
  • Medical Informatics
  • Software Development
  • User Experience Design

We’re passionate about making healthcare tools that are not only evidence-based but also easy to use and accessible to everyone.

References and Sources

Our tools are based on guidelines from reputable medical organizations including:

  • American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP)
  • Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
  • World Health Organization (WHO)
  • American Heart Association (AHA)
  • Infectious Diseases Society of America (IDSA)

Each tool includes specific references to the guidelines it implements.

Medical Disclaimer

MedAlgos tools are designed to support clinical decision-making but are not a substitute for professional medical judgment. Healthcare providers should use these tools as adjuncts to their clinical expertise and consider individual patient circumstances when making treatment decisions.
